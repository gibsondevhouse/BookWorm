# v1: Risks and Mitigations

- **Unforeseen performance issues:** Under real‑world usage patterns, new bottlenecks may appear. Mitigation: continue to profile and monitor after launch; allocate budget for emergency fixes.
- **Security vulnerabilities:** Despite audits, undiscovered vulnerabilities may exist. Mitigation: establish a responsible disclosure process and respond quickly to reports.
- **Installation complexity:** Self‑hosting may be challenging for some users. Mitigation: provide step‑by‑step guides, packaged release artifacts, and community support forums.
- **Scope pressure:** Stakeholders may push for additional features before v1 release. Mitigation: enforce feature freeze; schedule enhancements for post‑launch patches.
- **User adoption hurdles:** Authors and editors may find the product complex. Mitigation: invest in comprehensive documentation, tutorials and onboarding materials; collect and act on feedback.
- **Post‑Launch support load:** Unexpected bugs may require immediate attention. Mitigation: plan for a patch release schedule and maintain a hotfix branch.
